Privacy Policy

Last updated:

CAIO by Joshi Enterprises (“CAIO”, “we”, “our”, “us”) respects your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains what we collect, why we collect it, and how we use, share, and protect your information.

1) Information We Collect

  • Account — name, email, password hash (never your plain-text password).
  • Billing — plan, status, and payment references via our payment partners (we do not store full card numbers).
  • Usage — app events, basic device/browser data, approximate region, and diagnostics (to improve reliability and support).
  • Content — documents, prompts, and outputs you provide to deliver the service (analysis, exports, etc.).

2) How We Use Information

  • Provide, maintain, and improve CAIO and its features.
  • Process payments, prevent fraud/abuse, ensure acceptable use.
  • Customer support and service communications.
  • Compliance with legal obligations and enforcement of terms.

3) Sharing

We may share limited data with trusted service providers (e.g., hosting, analytics, payment) under contracts and appropriate safeguards. We do not sell personal data.

4) International Transfers

Your information may be processed in countries other than your own. We use appropriate transfer mechanisms and safeguards where required.

5) Data Retention

We retain information as long as necessary to provide the service, meet legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce agreements. You may request deletion where applicable.

6) Security

We use technical and organizational measures to protect information. However, no method of transmission or storage is 100% secure.

7) Your Choices & Rights

  • Access, correct, or delete your information where applicable.
  • Opt out of non-essential communications.
  • Contact us to exercise rights available under your local laws.

8) Children

CAIO is not directed to children under 13. If you believe a child provided us data, contact us to request deletion.

9) Changes

We may update this Policy from time to time. We will update the “Last updated” date and, where required, provide additional notice.
